I grew up in Drexel Hill, Delaware County.

I went to Monsignor Bonner, Temple, Villanova and Lycoming College. For the next 20 years, I was a UAW mechanic. After a decade building aircraft engines in Williamsport PA, I set my sights on a law career, and graduated from the Widener School of Law in 1998.

At age 45, I clerked for a Workers Compensation Judge in Philadelphia, and served with the Delaware County Public Defender’s Office, where I learned what it meant to defend the rights of everyday people in thorny circumstances.

In 2002, I launched my solo practice, briefly partnering with attorneys Hennessy & Bullen in Lansdowne. I cherish my freedom to choose my cases selectively, to personally directeach detail, and to help my clients at every step of the process to defend their rights and achieve their goals.

- Hugh McElhenney
